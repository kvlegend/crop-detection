Mathematical Blueprint

Convolutional Neural Network Architecture

This platform deploys a sequential deep computer vision pipeline. Below is the step-by-step breakdown of how the image values transition from raw pixels into semantic disease classification.

01
Normalizing Input

The 64x64 pixel image has RGB channel integers [0, 255] scaled to decimals [0, 1].

xnorm = x / 255.0
02
Convolution (Conv2D)

Slide weight kernels over pixels to calculate dot products, isolating edge and texture features.

f(x) = (w * x) + b
03
Activation (ReLU)

Applies Rectified Linear Unit activation to replace negative values with zero, introducing non-linearity.

f(x) = max(0, x)
04
Max Pooling 2D

Aggregates 2x2 grids, selecting the maximum local signal. Reduces parameters and increases spatial invariance.

pool(x) = max(X2x2)
05
Dense & Dropout

Flattens spatial dimensions into a single vector, mapping combinations to fully-connected layers.

y = σ(W · x + b)
06
Softmax Output

Normalizes outputs into a probability distribution over the five crop disease classes.

P(y=i) = ez_i / ∑ez_j
Reference Manual

Crop Disease & Pathogen Database

Review the structural characteristics, pathogen classifications, and visual patterns of standard crop leaf anomalies.

Fungal Pathogen

Tomato Late Blight

Phytophthora infestans

Produces dark, water-soaked irregular spots on leaf surfaces. Rapidly spreads in cool, moist climates, leading to complete plant collapse.

Fungal Pathogen

Corn Common Rust

Puccinia sorghi

Forms powdery, golden-brown to red pustules on both leaf surfaces. Leads to early leaf death and decreases crop yield significantly.

Ascomycete Fungal

Apple Scab

Venturia inaequalis

Creates olive-green to dark brown circular spots with velvety margins. Causes leaves to warp, yellow, and drop prematurely, damaging fruit.

Fungal Pathogen

Grape Black Rot

Guignardia bidwellii

Develops small, circular reddish-brown leaf spots with distinct dark borders. Spreads to fruit bunches, drying them into hard, black mummies.
